Dolphin

Dolphins are very social, living in groups that hunt and even play together. Large pods of dolphins can have 1,000 members or more.

  • Scientific Name: Delphinidae
  • Avarge Length: 5 meters
  • Avarge Weight: 5 tons
  • Avarge Lifespan: 50 years
  • Distribution: Worldwide

Dolphins are a widely distributed and diverse group of aquatic mammals. There are 40 extant species of dolphins. Dolphins, alongside other cetaceans, belong to the clade Cetartiodactyla with even-toed ungulates. They range in size from the 1.7 m (5.6 ft) long and 50 kg (110 lb) Maui's dolphin to the 9.5 m (31 ft) and 10 t (11 short tons) killer whale.
